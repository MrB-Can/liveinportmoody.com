**Aria** is at 110 Brew Street inside Suter Brook Village, the most walkable address in Port Moody. If the Heritage Mountain complexes represent the nature-and-schools end of the spectrum, Aria is the opposite: urban convenience, transit access, and a village-scale community right outside your door.

## Location

Suter Brook Village sits adjacent to Inlet Centre SkyTrain station and is built around a pedestrian-friendly retail and residential core.

- Shops, restaurants, and cafés, literally steps from your front door
- Inlet Centre SkyTrain: walk to downtown Vancouver in under 40 minutes
- Walking distance to Rocky Point Park, Port Moody Rec Centre, and the waterfront trail
- Breweries and the arts community that define Port Moody's identity are within a short walk

The walkability score here is one of the highest in the Tri-Cities. If you're a regular transit user, this location essentially eliminates car dependence for daily errands and commuting.

## The Community

Aria townhomes are part of the larger Suter Brook development, meaning residents benefit from the shared infrastructure and amenity investment of the broader village. The complex is well-maintained and attracts a mix of downsizers, professionals, and couples.

Homes feature:

- Modern architecture with a clean, contemporary exterior
- Efficient layouts designed for low-maintenance living
- Private patios or terraces
- Secure parking and storage
- Open-concept living spaces with contemporary kitchens

## Who This Is Right For

Aria suits buyers who specifically want the Port Moody lifestyle but aren't looking for a yard, a basement, or Heritage Mountain schools. Downsizers moving out of a detached home often land here: you get ownership, private space, and walkability without maintenance headaches.

Professionals commuting to downtown Vancouver, Burnaby, or the Broadway corridor find the SkyTrain access genuinely transformative for daily quality of life.

## What to Know Before Buying

Suter Brook is a strata-heavy environment. Expect strata fees that reflect both the building upkeep and the shared commercial/residential infrastructure. Understanding what your fees cover (and what they don't) is important before making an offer.

The urban village format also means occasional noise from the commercial activity below and nearby. For buyers prioritizing quiet suburban life, Aria isn't the right fit. For buyers who want energy and convenience, it usually exceeds expectations.
