**Cobalt Sky** is located at 55 Hawthorn Drive in Heritage Woods, a Port Moody neighbourhood that often appeals to families looking for space, school-access questions to verify, and access to nature.

## Location and Community

Heritage Woods sits in the elevated eastern portion of Port Moody, bordered by forested greenspace and trail networks.

- Close to **Aspenwood Elementary**, **Eagle Mountain Middle**, and **Heritage Woods Secondary** — verify current catchment and school assignment with SD43 by exact address
- Easy access to parks, trails, and outdoor recreation
- Convenient bus routes to Port Moody Centre and SkyTrain; Heritage Mountain and Newport Village within a short drive

## About the Development

Built in **2006–2007** by **Parklane Homes**, Cobalt Sky consists of approximately **95 low-rise townhouse units**.

A key distinction: Cobalt Sky is **freehold strata**, meaning each owner holds title to their unit and the land beneath it. This gives a bit more of a true ownership feel compared to leasehold or bare-land strata arrangements.

The architecture is restrained and classic — these homes don't have the flashy modern exterior of newer developments, but they're solidly built with layouts that work well for real family life.

## Home Features

Units at Cobalt Sky are known for practical, spacious designs:

✅ **3–4 bedrooms with 2–4 bathrooms** — enough room for families or shared living
✅ **Open main floors** with fireplaces and living/dining areas that flow well
✅ **Gourmet kitchens** with granite counters, maple cabinets, and generous storage
✅ **Hardwood or quality laminate flooring** with 9-foot ceilings throughout
✅ **Fenced patios or yards** for kids, pets, and outdoor living
✅ **Double or tandem garages** plus additional driveway parking

Several units include **view decks and greenbelt outlooks**, taking advantage of the natural surroundings. Three-storey layouts mean the top floor primary suite typically has genuine privacy from the rest of the house.

## Lifestyle and Appeal

Cobalt Sky attracts families who want a townhome that feels like a house — generous square footage, private outdoor space, and a quiet neighbourhood with established infrastructure.

The Heritage Woods location is notably calm. You're not walking to SkyTrain from here, but you're also not dealing with the density and noise of Port Moody Centre. For families with school-age children, the trade-off is usually an easy one.

## What to Know Before Buying

Cobalt Sky is now approximately 18–19 years old. Strata depreciation reports, contingency reserve fund status, and upcoming capital expenditures are worth reviewing carefully before any purchase. Parklane built well, but buildings of this age typically face roof, window, and mechanical system planning within the next 5–10 years.

The freehold strata designation is a genuine positive — it adds flexibility and ownership clarity compared to some other structures in the area.
