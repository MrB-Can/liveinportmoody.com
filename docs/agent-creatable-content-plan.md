## PUBLIC-SOURCE CONTENT

Content that can be created using publicly available, verifiable sources:

1. **School Information** (/neighbourhoods, /local-life)
   - Source: BC School District official websites
   - Safe to add: School names, catchment areas, ratings
   - Don't invent: Test scores, specific rankings

2. **Transit and Commute Information** (/neighbourhoods, /local-life)
   - Source: TransLink, City of Port Moody
   - Safe to add: Transit routes, commute times (approximate)
   - Don't invent: Future plans, routing changes

3. **Park and Trail Information** (/local-life)
   - Source: City of Port Moody Parks, public trail maps
   - Safe to add: Park names, trail names, amenities
   - Don't invent: Condition, usage data, comparisons to other cities

4. **Local Business Directory** (/featured-businesses, /local-life)
   - Source: Business directories, Google Maps (public data only)
   - Safe to add: Business names, categories, locations
   - Don't invent: Reviews, ratings (unless from verified sources)

5. **Real Estate Definitions and Processes** (glossary, guides)
   - Source: Real estate boards, industry standards
   - Safe to add: Standard definitions, process steps
   - Don't invent: Market-specific advice without local knowledge

## CONSTRAINTS

**Always follow:**
- No invented facts, reviews, awards, or market claims
- No fake business features or events
- No client stories without explicit consent
- No unverified credentials or awards
- All frameworks use existing site data or standard real estate practices
- All public-source content verified before publication

**Exception scenarios:**
- Glossaries can define terms even if not all apply to Port Moody yet
- Decision frameworks can include options even if not all have examples on site
- Checklists can suggest professional review (legal, tax, inspection) for matters requiring expertise

---

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

All agent-creatable content identified above uses ONLY:
1. Existing neighbourhood/property data already on site
2. Standard real estate practices and terminology
3. Logical decision frameworks based on property characteristics
4. Public-source information with verified sources

None require:
- Owner approval or private information
- Invented facts or market claims
- Client testimonials or permission
- Unverified awards or credentials
- Future-looking predictions or guarantees
