## Shot Priority Order (Across All Three Neighbourhoods)

Shoot in this order if you only have one session or limited time. The hero shots matter most — everything else is secondary.

| Priority | Shot | Neighbourhood | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Waterfront / Rocky Point boardwalk — golden hour | Moody Centre | Highest visual impact; strongest buyer draw |
| 2 | Tree-lined residential street — morning | Glenayre | Defines the neighbourhood; easy to execute |
| 3 | Residential street with newer homes — morning | Heritage Woods | Needed for any public launch |
| 4 | OG/social crops from each hero | All three | Batch from hero files; no reshoots needed |
| 5 | Moody Centre — Brewery Row exterior | Moody Centre | Strong lifestyle signal |
| 6 | Glenayre — housing character close-up | Glenayre | Supports hero |
| 7 | Heritage Woods — trail or natural edge | Heritage Woods | Supports hero |
| 8 | Moody Centre — park or green space | Moody Centre | Secondary content |
| 9 | All remaining supporting shots | All three | Fill in once heroes are captured |

Do not chase supporting shots if the hero is not yet captured. Heroes first, always.

## Exact Shot List by Neighbourhood

### Heritage Woods

**Hero (required)**

- A forward-facing view down a characteristic Heritage Woods street
- Newer streetscape visible: sidewalks, mixed townhouse/detached rooflines, street trees
- Natural backdrop (treeline or open sky) in upper third of frame
- No identifiable residents, no parked cars as the primary subject
- **Shoot:** Morning, 8–10 am, east-facing or south-facing street
- **Lens:** 24mm–35mm equivalent (wide-normal; keeps streets from compressing)
- **Crop:** 16:9 hero and 1200×630 OG

**Supporting Shot A — Street character**
- Wider residential street showing consistent newer streetscape
- Emphasis on sidewalks, front yards, uniform-era homes
- No specific address legible; no private driveways featured
- **Lens:** 35mm equivalent, f/8 for depth

**Supporting Shot B — Park or green space**
- Community park or open green area within Heritage Woods
- Open lawn, benches, or playground equipment if present (no children)
- Wide angle to show the green space scale and surrounding homes
- **Lens:** 24mm equivalent

**Supporting Shot C — Trail access**
- Multi-use path or walking trail within or at the edge of Heritage Woods
- Show the path surface, surrounding trees, natural light
- Should look local — not a generic trail photo
- **Lens:** 35mm equivalent, slight foreground path leading into frame

**Supporting Shot D — Housing style**
- Medium composition of newer-build Heritage Woods homes
- Mixed-material facades, covered entries, newer construction detail
- From public sidewalk only; no private yard or windows
- No address or house numbers legible
- **Lens:** 50mm–85mm equivalent (compresses slightly; cleaner architectural detail)

**Supporting Shot E — Natural edge or backdrop**
- Heritage Woods meeting the forested backdrop or hillside treeline
- Shoot looking outward from the residential area into the natural edge
- Not a photo of specific homes — context of nature-adjacent character
- **Lens:** 35mm equivalent

### Moody Centre

**Hero (required)**

- View from Rocky Point Park boardwalk looking toward the inlet and mountains
- Water visible in lower or mid frame; mountains in background; sky and clouds in upper third
- Late afternoon light (5–7 pm summer); inlet reflects light; mountains gain warmth
- No children in foreground; no identifiable individuals close to camera
- **Shoot:** Golden hour, west-facing from boardwalk
- **Lens:** 24mm–35mm equivalent (wide; captures water + mountains + foreground path)
- **Crop:** 16:9 hero and 1200×630 OG; keep water and mountains visible in both

**Supporting Shot A — Waterfront / Rocky Point**
- Same area as hero or adjacent boardwalk section
- Tighter on the path, benches, waterfront edge, or inlet surface
- A different framing from the hero — landscape crop but closer
- **Lens:** 35mm equivalent

**Supporting Shot B — Urban street character**
- St. Johns Street or a mixed-use block with ground-floor commercial and mid-rise above
- Midday or afternoon; active street context; people visible in distance (not close)
- Captures walkable urban feel that buyers care about
- **Lens:** 35mm–50mm equivalent; slight distance to show building height and street scale

**Supporting Shot C — Brewery Row exterior**
- Exterior of Brewers Row or a single brewery storefront from the public sidewalk
- Captures lifestyle amenity without endorsing a specific business
- Context shot — building, signage, street-level character
- Do not photograph faces of patrons or staff
- **Lens:** 35mm equivalent

**Supporting Shot D — Transit / station context**
- Moody Centre SkyTrain station exterior or station plaza entrance
- From public sidewalk or plaza; not from the platform (may require TransLink permission)
- Shows the transit connection buyers evaluate
- **Lens:** 24mm–35mm equivalent; shoot with station structure in frame

**Supporting Shot E — Park lifestyle context**
- Rocky Point Park open lawn, trees, water in background
- Wider shot showing the park's scale and openness
- No children; no individuals in close foreground
- **Lens:** 24mm equivalent; shoot from lawn level looking toward water

### Glenayre

**Hero (required)**

- A long, tree-lined residential street in Glenayre's core
- Mature deciduous or mixed canopy overhead; shadows dappled on pavement
- Street receding into greenery; low camera angle to emphasize canopy
- No cars as primary subject; empty or near-empty street preferred
- **Shoot:** Morning, 8–11 am; OR autumn golden hour if trees have colour (October)
- **Lens:** 24mm–35mm equivalent; wide to capture full canopy height
- **Crop:** 16:9 hero and 1200×630 OG; depth of street and canopy must both be visible

**Supporting Shot A — Tree canopy close-up**
- Looking up into or along the tree canopy from street level
- Emphasizes the mature green character that Glenayre buyers value
- May double as a section card image
- **Lens:** 24mm equivalent; low angle

**Supporting Shot B — Housing style**
- Detached Glenayre home from the public sidewalk
- Established landscaping, mature front yard trees, single-family character
- No specific address or house numbers; no rear yard or windows
- Medium composition — not too tight, not a full street scene
- **Lens:** 50mm–85mm equivalent

**Supporting Shot C — Quiet street / neighbourhood calm**
- Wide view of a Glenayre residential street with no traffic and minimal activity
- Should feel calm, private, suburban, and mature
- This is a deliberate contrast to Moody Centre's urban bustle
- **Lens:** 35mm equivalent; straight-on framing

**Supporting Shot D — Park or neighbourhood green space**
- Local park, open field, or green area within or adjacent to Glenayre
- Open space, trees, and perhaps benches
- No children; no private yards
- **Lens:** 35mm equivalent; shoot from open area looking toward trees

**Supporting Shot E — Forested edge (optional)**
- Glenayre homes at the edge of forested terrain
- Shoot from a public vantage point looking toward the natural boundary
- Shows the privacy and nature-adjacent character
- **Lens:** 35mm–50mm equivalent

**Seasonal note:** If shooting in October, the Glenayre hero with autumn canopy colour is the strongest version of this shot. If scheduling permits, prioritise an October session for Glenayre specifically. Spring and summer shoots are fine for supporting shots.

## Lens Suggestions

You do not need multiple lenses. A single 24–70mm zoom covers every shot in this list. Primes produce better rendering if you have them.

| Focal length (equivalent) | Best for | Shots |
|---|---|---|
| 24mm | Wide context, street scenes with sky or canopy, parks | Moody Centre hero, Glenayre hero, park shots |
| 35mm | General purpose, streets, lifestyle | Most supporting shots across all three |
| 50mm | Slightly compressed; building facades, housing style | Heritage Woods housing, Glenayre housing |
| 85mm–105mm | Compressed background separation, architectural detail | Optional detail shots if shooting heritage-style buildings |

Avoid ultra-wide (below 20mm) for street scenes unless you want intentional distortion. Avoid telephoto (above 135mm) unless you are specifically compressing a distant treeline or mountain backdrop.

## Best Time of Day

| Neighbourhood | Hero session | Supporting shots |
|---|---|---|
| **Heritage Woods** | Morning 8–10 am | Morning or overcast midday |
| **Moody Centre** | Late afternoon / golden hour 5–7 pm (summer) | Midday works for street and station shots |
| **Glenayre** | Morning 8–11 am | Morning or overcast midday; autumn if possible |

**Overcast days** are underrated. A bright overcast sky gives even, soft light with no harsh shadows. Works well for Heritage Woods housing shots and Glenayre street scenes. Avoid dark, rainy overcast that makes streets look grim.

**Avoid:** Midday on clear days for canopy or waterfront shots. Harsh shadows and blown-out sky make both look worse.

## Parking and Access Notes

### Heritage Woods
- **Parking:** Ample free street parking throughout the neighbourhood; arrive at a quiet residential block
- **Access:** All shots are from public streets and sidewalks; no parks require a parking lot
- **Orientation:** The neighbourhood is a planned development — streets are regular and easy to navigate; use Google Maps street view in advance to scout candidate streets

### Moody Centre
- **Parking:** Rocky Point Park has a paid parking lot (small fee on weekends and summer afternoons); St. Johns Street has metered street parking
- **Access:** Boardwalk is fully public; all trail and park areas are publicly accessible
- **Busy periods:** Rocky Point Park is very busy on summer weekend afternoons — arrive early (before 9 am) for empty foregrounds, or accept some background pedestrians
- **Brewery Row:** Public sidewalk; no access required; avoid peak hours (Fri/Sat evening) if you want less foot traffic in frame

### Glenayre
- **Parking:** Free residential street parking throughout; pick a spot near the candidate street
- **Access:** All shots are from public streets and sidewalks
- **Scouting:** The neighbourhood is compact; do a 10-minute walking scout on arrival before shooting

## Composition Guidance

**Street shots:**
- Shoot from slightly below standard eye height (lower camera, more imposing canopy and sky)
- Use the road or sidewalk as a leading line into the frame
- Keep horizon level — adjust in post if needed, but get it close in camera
- Place the main interest (treeline, roofline, park entrance) at the one-third line, not dead centre

**Waterfront (Moody Centre):**
- Water should occupy the lower third or mid-frame; mountains behind; sky above
- A path, railing, or boardwalk edge in the foreground gives depth
- If shooting at golden hour, expose for the sky — let the foreground go slightly dark; lift in post

**Canopy shots (Glenayre):**
- Stand in the middle of the road (when safe; no traffic) for a symmetrical canopy shot
- Or stand on the sidewalk and shoot along the road from a low angle
- Morning light coming from the side makes canopy shots more dimensional

**Housing style:**
- Avoid shooting directly into the sun (flare and loss of detail on facade)
- Shoot slightly to the side, not dead-on, to give the building some dimension
- Leave breathing room around the house; you do not need to fill the frame

**General rules:**
- Horizons level
- Foreground to mid-ground to background — something in all three if possible
- No lens flare unless deliberate
- Check all four corners of the frame before shooting

## Privacy Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **No children, ever.** Children's faces, backs, silhouettes, or identifiable clothing. If a child is in the frame, do not use the shot. Wait for the area to clear.

2. **No identifiable individuals in close foreground.** Distant, unrecognisable pedestrians in the background are acceptable context. Anyone close enough to be recognised is not acceptable without written consent.

3. **No private property interiors.** Through windows, over fences, into backyards. Stay on the public sidewalk or road.

4. **No specific addresses.** No house numbers in foreground. No mailbox numbers. Licence plates: avoid foreground; incidental background plates in a wider street scene are acceptable.

5. **No school buildings.** Do not photograph Glenayre Elementary or any school building. The guide uses SD43 address-verification language; a photo of a school implies an exact catchment. Do not undermine that caution.

6. **No photos outside the neighbourhood boundary.** No Newport Village or Rocky Point photos used for Heritage Woods. No Heritage Mountain photos used for Glenayre. Label all files correctly at import.

7. **No construction sites or private development areas.** Stick to public streets, parks, and trails.

## Crop Requirements

Export each hero at all three sizes from one source file. Crop in editing software (Lightroom, Capture One, etc.) before exporting WebP.

| Use | Dimensions | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hero (16:9) | 1920×1080 minimum; 2560×1440 preferred | Landscape only; canopy or mountains should be visible |
| Section / card (4:3) | 800×600 minimum | Can be a tighter crop from the hero, or a separate shot |
| OG / social (1:91:1) | 1200×630 | Crop that reads well at thumbnail size; avoid placing key subject at edges |
| Index thumbnail (1:1) | 400×400 | Only if the index page adds square thumbnails; crop from hero center |

**Colour space:** sRGB. Export in sRGB even if you shot in a wider gamut.

**Compression target:**
- Hero: under 200 KB
- Supporting shots: under 100 KB
- OG image: under 80 KB

Use Squoosh, ImageOptim, or your editor's WebP export settings. Do not export at quality 100 — quality 80–85 is indistinguishable at these sizes.

**Do not:** upscale small files. If a source file is too small, reshoot rather than upscale.

## Upload and Review Workflow

### Step 1 — Back up before anything else
- Copy camera card to local drive
- Copy to a second location (external drive or cloud) before importing or editing
- Confirm backup exists before formatting or using the card again

### Step 2 — Import and rename
- Import to your editing software (Lightroom, Capture One, or folder structure)
- Rename all files immediately using the convention above
- Tag files by neighbourhood and role

### Step 3 — Cull
- First pass: delete technical failures (blur, overexposed, privacy issues)
- Second pass: select the best candidate per shot type (hero, street, park, etc.)
- Aim for one approved file per role, not a pool of alternatives

### Step 4 — Edit
- Adjust exposure, contrast, and white balance
- Level the horizon
- Correct distortion if shooting at wide focal lengths
- Do not over-process: neighbourhood photos should look like the neighbourhood, not a magazine render
- Do not add AI sky replacements, composites, or HDR merges that misrepresent the scene

### Step 5 — Export source files
- Export full-resolution TIFF or high-quality JPEG as archival source before web export
- Keep source files permanently

### Step 6 — Export web files
- Export WebP at the crop sizes listed above
- Name files per convention
- Confirm file sizes are within targets

### Step 7 — Review before commit
- Open each WebP in a browser at the intended display size
- Confirm: sharp, level, correctly cropped, no privacy issues, no misleading elements
- Confirm: correctly named, correct neighbourhood, correct role

### Step 8 — Hand off or commit
- Deliver files to web implementation (developer hand-off, or direct commit to `/public/images/neighbourhoods/`)
- Update the Photo Registry
- Update `neighbourhoodGuides.ts` or the route page to reference the new image path (separate implementation task)

## Photo Registry Update Checklist

After each approved shot is exported and ready for use, update the Photo Registry with the following fields for each file:

**One entry per role per neighbourhood:**

```
- Title: Heritage Woods — hero street shot
  Neighbourhood: Heritage Woods
  Page: /neighbourhoods/heritage-woods
  Role: hero
  Status: approved / pending-review / needed
  File: neighbourhood-heritage-woods-hero.webp
  Dimensions: 1920×1080
  OG export: neighbourhood-heritage-woods-og.webp
  Desired shot: Residential street, morning light, newer homes with treeline backdrop
  Season shot: [Spring / Summer / Autumn / Winter]
  Date shot: [YYYY-MM-DD]
  Permissions: Public space — no consent required
  Priority: high
  Notes: [Any relevant notes]
```

Repeat for every file. Do not commit files to the repo without a Photo Registry entry.

**Status values:**

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `needed` | Shot required; not yet taken |
| `shot` | Taken but not yet edited or approved |
| `edited` | Edited; pending final review |
| `approved` | Approved; ready for implementation |
| `live` | In production on the site |
| `replaced` | Replaced by a newer shot; archive original |
